# Editorial tone — truesight.me and agroverse.shop

Voice and style guide for the two public-facing publications in this
workspace. When drafting a blog post, page, product description, or any
copy that will live on either site, read the relevant section below and
match the existing register. **Do not invent a new house style** — both
sites have years of accumulated voice; new copy that breaks the tonal
contract reads as imported, even if every sentence is technically fine.

Companion to `CMO_SETH_GODIN.md` (strategic frame: who-for, what-change,
remarkability). This file is about *texture* — the sentence rhythm, the
vocabulary palette, the way an author's hand looks on the page.

---

## 1. truesight.me/blog — long-form essays for operators and aligned outsiders

**What it is:** TrueSight DAO's public blog. Roughly two posts a week as
of mid-2026, indexed at `/blog/`. Each post is 2,000–6,000 words.

**Who it's for:** Operators (people running their own DAOs, supply
chains, multi-agent systems), curious aligned outsiders (researchers,
practitioners thinking about regenerative or AI infrastructure), and
existing contributors who want to see their world named back to them.
**Not for:** VC pitch-deck audiences, generalist tech press, retail
customers.

### 1.1 Voice signature

- **Concrete data point in the opener, then a paradox.** "67 stores are
  sitting in our partner warmup queue. Zero emails went out this week."
  Then the body resolves why.
- **Short declarative sentences mixed with one longer rhythmic
  paragraph.** Land moments of stillness — single-sentence paragraphs
  used as punctuation.
- **Plumbing-and-soul register.** A single paragraph can move from
  `RSA-2048 signed rows dispatched through Edgar` to `something left
  for the next generation to grow up to … aka LOVE`. The contrast is
  the voice. Pure technical reads as dry; pure philosophical reads as
  vapor. Hold both at once.
- **Quiet contrarianism.** Almost every post argues against the obvious
  industry take. "We discover protocols, not specify them." "Stop doing
  what the machine can do." "The model isn't the moat." Never angry,
  never strident — confident understatement.

### 1.2 Headline conventions

Common patterns observed in the live index:

- **Concept + colon + plain-English expansion.** "The Mycelial Economy:
  libraries, borders, and the quiet return of an older trade."
- **Evocative phrase, no subtitle.** "The Do Nothing Society", "Where 道
  Integrates with DAO."
- **Methodology declaration.** "Plug-and-play architecture: why every
  service reads from one sheet."
- **Incident framing.** "Digital infrastructure: AWS flagged our
  account — what we did, and why Cypher-Defense exists."

Avoid: corporate-newsletter-style headlines ("How TrueSight is leading
the way in…"), question-as-clickbait ("Is your DAO ready for…"),
generic-trend headlines ("The future of multi-agent AI is here").

### 1.3 Byline and authorship

- **Always named.** Either `by Gary Teh, long time contributor` or
  `by Claude (Anthropic)` / `by Kimi (Moonshot AI)` / `by Grok (xAI)` /
  etc. LLM bylines are normal and treated as first-class.
- **"long time contributor"** is the preferred descriptor for Gary —
  never "founder," "CEO," or "co-founder." This is a hard rule, not a
  stylistic preference.
- For LLM-authored posts, name the company in parentheses so the
  reader can place the model's training lineage.

### 1.4 Structural conventions

- **H2 section headings** break the essay into 3–7 named arcs.
  Headings themselves carry the argument: "The DAO ledger" / "The 道
  ledger" / "Where they meet."
- **One hero image** at the top — typically a `.svg` from
  `/assets/blog/` showing a visual metaphor with two contrasting
  elements (warm organic vs geometric grid; two mirrors joined by a
  seam; a five-rung ladder).
- **Tables** for compare/contrast moments ("AI handles | Humans
  handle"). Used sparingly — never as the bulk of the post.
- **Inline links to internal artifacts** — `[Edgar](https://truesight.me/edgar)`,
  GitHub repos, specific spreadsheets. The links *are* part of the
  argument; the reader can verify every claim against a primary source.
- **Series tags** as pill badges above the title: "Field Signals · #1",
  "Operator Notes · #3", etc. Use when launching a series that will
  have at least 3 entries; don't create singleton series.

### 1.5 Vocabulary palette

**Lean into:**
- `ledger`, `signed`, `chain`, `cron`, `health check` (plumbing)
- `attunement`, `zeitgeist`, `soul`, `LOVE` (in caps), `the why`,
  `relational health` (soul)
- `discover`, `convene`, `route around`, `quietly`, `on purpose`,
  `mechanically`, `by design`
- 道, mycelial, regenerative, kintsugi, "people like us" — borrowed
  terms used precisely, never as decoration

**Avoid:**
- "Disrupt," "synergy," "leverage" (as a verb), "ecosystem play," "stack,"
  "north star" — VC/SaaS vocabulary
- "Game-changer," "next-generation," "revolutionary" — superlatives
- "Founder," "founding member," "team member" — see memory feedback
- "We're excited to announce" — never. Open with a fact, not an
  emotion claim about ourselves.

### 1.6 Tone calibration

| Axis                          | Target                                              |
|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Formality                     | Conversational-but-considered. Like a long letter.  |
| Sales pressure                | Zero. The post does not ask for anything.           |
| First-person                  | "We" for the DAO; "I" rare and only when essential. |
| Reader address                | "You" used to invite, never to instruct.            |
| Closing move                  | Contemplative pull-back, occasionally a quiet ask.  |
| Length                        | 2,000–4,000 words is the sweet spot.                |

### 1.7 Strongest opening hooks (study these before drafting)

- "67 stores are sitting in our partner warmup queue. Zero emails
  went out this week. The cron is healthy."
  *— Where 道 Integrates with DAO*
- "After reading the corrected ledger, the supply chain chats, and
  months of philosophy threads, the answer is simple and contrarian:
  stop doing anything that the chatbot can do."
  *— The Do Nothing Society*

The pattern: a measurable fact + a counterfactual + a setup that
demands the rest of the essay to resolve. Earn the read in the first
two sentences.

## 2. agroverse.shop — commerce site, place-anchored supply chain story

**What it is:** Agroverse's retail and partner-discovery site. Mix of
product pages, farm profiles, cooperative profiles, "Cacao Journeys"
narrative routes, and an episodic "Bean to Bliss" blog.

**Who it's for:** Consumers buying ceremonial cacao, potential retail
partners evaluating us, supply-chain-curious supporters who want to
follow specific beans from a specific farm to their cup.
**Not for:** Engineers, DAO governance audience, philosophy readers —
that audience reads truesight.me.

### 2.1 Voice signature

- **Place-first.** Every entity is anchored to a real geography:
  "Bahia, Brazil," "Pará's Amazon," "Itacaré," "Florianópolis,"
  "Founderhaus." The reader should be able to point at a map.
- **People named, not abstracted.** Farms are named after the farmers
  who run them: "Fazenda Analuana, operated by Ana Luana," "Oscar's
  Farm," "Paulo's Farm," "Santos's Melanger." First name + farm name
  is the canonical form for retail partners (see memory rule).
- **Warm, slightly more descriptive sentences than truesight.me.**
  Compound clauses joined by em-dashes are normal: "From Washington's
  borderlands near Canada to the California desert near Mexico, this
  route follows a path reminiscent of the Pacific Crest Trail, linking
  partner venues for tastings, retail, and regenerative commerce."
- **Collective voice.** "We," "Our cacao," "Our partners." No
  individual bylines on product or farm pages. The blog (Bean to
  Bliss) carries Santos, Kirsten, etc. as the on-camera subjects.

### 2.2 Headline conventions

- **Place + product:** "Premium cacao from Brazil—Pará's Amazon and
  Bahia's Atlantic Forest"
- **Person + action:** "Santos's Winnowing & Cracking — From Roasted
  Beans to Cacao Nibs", "Kirsten (KiKi's Cocoa) makes hot chocolate"
- **Evocative + grounded:** "The Brazilian Drift", "Savor the
  Regeneration", "Walk the paths where our cacao travels"
- **Episode numbering for series:** "Bean to Bliss — Episode 7
  (transcript)"

Avoid: TrueSight-flavored philosophy headlines. "The Mycelial Economy"
would feel out of place on Agroverse.

### 2.3 Structural conventions

- **Product descriptions follow a fixed shape:** product type + dash +
  farm + dash + country + dash + year + parenthesized weight.
  *"Ceremonial Cacao – La do Sitio Farm, Pará Brazil, 2024 (200g)"*
- **Farm pages** open with one paragraph that hits: who runs it, where
  it is, what they produce, and how they fit into the regenerative
  story. Then "Farm Highlights" + "Shipments from This Farm" (AGL
  shipment IDs as artifacts: AGL5, AGL7, AGL0) + "Farm Location" (map).
- **Cooperative pages** mirror the farm-page shape but with "Member
  Farms" replacing the single-farm focus.
- **Cacao Journey pages** read like travel literature — a sequence of
  destinations linked by a unifying experience theme (regenerative
  cacao, capoeira, samba, jungle adventures).
- **Footer mantra (every page):** *"Regenerating our Amazon rainforest,
  One Cacao at a time"* + phone `415-300-0019`. Don't omit, don't
  rewrite.

### 2.4 Vocabulary palette

**Lean into:**
- `regenerative`, `harvest-to-door`, `traceability`, `single-estate`,
  `ceremonial`, `Bean to Bliss`, `partner venues`, `tasting`,
  `cooperative`, `small-scale`
- Farmer first names, farm full names, place names in Portuguese
  ("Fazenda" not "farm" when it's the proper noun)
- Process verbs: `roast`, `winnow`, `temper`, `melanger`, `craft`,
  `route`, `anchor`, `connect`

**Avoid:**
- DAO/blockchain/Edgar/ledger terminology in customer-facing copy. If
  it appears, link out to truesight.me for the technical reader.
- "Premium" is OK once per page, not as a recurring adjective.
- Sales urgency ("limited time," "only 5 left") — undermines the
  harvest-cycle truthfulness the site relies on.

### 2.5 Tone calibration

| Axis                          | Target                                              |
|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| Formality                     | Warm-professional. Like a thoughtful retail catalog. |
| Sales pressure                | Light — present the product clearly, let the story sell. |
| First-person                  | "We" / "Our." Never "I."                            |
| Reader address                | "You" to invite into the experience, not to instruct. |
| Closing move                  | The footer mantra functions as the closing. Body copy ends on a forward gesture (visit, taste, walk). |
| Length                        | 200–600 words for farm/coop/product pages; longer for Cacao Journeys narrative. |

## 3. Decision rule — where does a piece of copy belong?

| Topic                                                | Goes on               |
|------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------|
| Methodology, architecture, ops, governance, philosophy | **truesight.me/blog** |
| Multi-LLM orchestration, AI infrastructure essays    | **truesight.me/blog** |
| Specific farm, farmer, cooperative profile           | **agroverse.shop/farms** or `/cooperatives` |
| Product (cacao, chocolate, nibs)                     | **agroverse.shop** product page |
| Travel route or experiential journey                 | **agroverse.shop/cacao-journeys** |
| Process pedagogy (roasting, winnowing, melangeur)    | **agroverse.shop/blog** ("Bean to Bliss") |
| Incident postmortem, security disclosure             | **truesight.me/blog** |
| Press/PR-style "announcement"                        | Neither. Both sites refuse the announcement genre. Rewrite as a methodology essay (truesight) or a story (agroverse). |

When a piece could plausibly live on either, default to **truesight.me**
if the reader needs to understand *how* something works to care, and
**agroverse.shop** if the reader needs to *taste, see, or visit*
something to care.

## 4. Pre-publish checklist

Before shipping any new post or page:

- [ ] Read the existing 2–3 most recent pieces in the same surface area
      out loud. Does the new draft sound like a sibling, or like an
      import from elsewhere?
- [ ] Headline test: does it match one of the patterns in §1.2 or §2.2?
- [ ] Byline correct? (truesight.me: named human or named LLM;
      agroverse.shop: collective "we" unless it's a Bean to Bliss
      episode featuring a specific person)
- [ ] Vocabulary scan for banned terms (§1.5, §2.4)
- [ ] Does the closing move match the site's convention? (truesight:
      contemplative pull-back; agroverse: forward gesture + mantra)
- [ ] If it links into the other site, are the cross-links sane?
      (truesight should never push a sales CTA; agroverse should never
      open with DAO jargon)
