## Overview

Every food item imported into China requires the manufacturer to have a **GACC registration number** from China Customs (General Administration of Customs of China). One product, one registration. Without it, customs clearance cannot proceed.

**Effective regulation:** GACC Decree No. 280 (effective **June 1, 2026**), replacing Decree No. 248. Existing valid registrations carry over.

**Key change for cocoa:** Under Decree No. 280, **unroasted cocoa beans** have been **removed from the "Official Recommendation" list** (reduced from 18 to 17 categories). This means cocoa/cacao products now follow **Pathway 2: Self-Application Registration** — faster and simpler than before. No recommendation letter from MAPA needed.

## D. GACC Registration Process — Step by Step

### Step 1: Determine Registration Pathway

Under Decree No. 280 (effective June 1, 2026):

| Product | NCM | Pathway |
|---------|-----|---------|
| Unroasted cocoa beans / almonds | 1801.00.00 | **Self-application** (Pathway 2) — removed from official recommendation list |
| Cacao nibs, mass, husk | 1803.10.00 | Likely **Self-application** — confirm with GACC consultant |
| Chocolate bars (not filled) | 1806.32.00 | **Self-application** (Pathway 2) |
| Cacao tea | 2106.90.00 | Likely **Self-application** — confirm with GACC consultant |

### Step 2: Register on CIFER System

1. Go to **cifer.singlewindow.cn**
2. Create an account as an overseas manufacturer
3. Submit the following documents:
   - Business license / CNPJ registration certificate (translated to Chinese)
   - Food safety management system documentation (HACCP, GMP, or equivalent)
   - Product list with descriptions
   - Compliance declaration
4. GACC reviews and issues registration number (18-digit code)
5. **Timeline:** 3–6 months for first-time registration

### Step 3: Prepare Chinese Labels

Once GACC number is obtained, prepare bilingual labels for each product. The GACC number must be printed on the label. Each distinct product needs its own label.

### Step 4: Ship with Complete Documentation

Present all documents from Section C at customs clearance in Dongguan.

## H. Regulatory References

- **GACC Decree No. 280** — Regulations on Registration of Overseas Manufacturers of Imported Food (effective June 1, 2026)
- **GACC Announcement No. 27 (2026)** — Implementation rules for Decree No. 280
- **CIFER System:** cifer.singlewindow.cn
- **Brazil competent authority:** MAPA (Ministério da Agricultura, Pecuária e Abastecimento)
- **Brazil is on the approved list** of countries that can export unroasted cocoa beans to China
- **Packaging:** Must comply with GB 4806 series (food contact materials). Heat-sealed food-grade bags are sufficient — vacuum sealing is not required by Chinese regulations.
