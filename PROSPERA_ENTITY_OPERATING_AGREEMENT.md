**WHEREAS**, the DAO operates the **Agroverse** initiative — a regenerative-agriculture trade bridge that moves verified cacao and agroforestry produce from small-scale Northern-Brazilian farming cooperatives and producers to conscious buyers abroad — and, as a separate initiative, the **SunMint** regenerative-finance (ReFi) / carbon-sequestration program;

**WHEREAS**, on 2026-07-01 the DAO's General Assembly, through its GitHub-and-digital-signature governance process, **merged Proposal #18** authorizing the incorporation of this Próspera ZEDE entity to coordinate, finance, and book the Agroverse trade flow and to operate a triangular trade structure (Brazil Export Partners → Company (Próspera) → U.S. Import Partner); the Members adopt the looser **partner-network** structure set out herein in lieu of a Company-owned Brazilian subsidiary, thereby avoiding the unresolved foreign-quotaholder question flagged for counsel in `BRAZIL_EXPORT_ENTITY_BRIEF.md`;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, the Members adopt this Operating Agreement as the governing internal instrument ("by-laws") of the Company.

## Article I — Definitions

1.3 **"TDG"** — TrueSight DAO Governance Tokens, the accounting units recorded on the Main Ledger (defined below) and issued per the Rubric. TDG are ledger entries, not on-chain tokens.

1.4 **"Main Ledger"** — the canonical TrueSight DAO Google Sheet at spreadsheet ID `1GE7PUq-UT6x2rBN-Q2ksogbWpgyuh2SaxJyG_uEK6PU`, including its **Contributors contact information**, **Governors**, **Currencies**, and **offchain transactions** tabs, together with any successor system of record adopted by DAO vote.

## Article III — Purpose, Mission Alignment, and Powers

3.1 **Purpose.** The Company is organized to:
(a) coordinate and contract with an independent network of Trade Partners — Brazilian Export Partners on the origin side and the U.S. (and future) Import Partner(s) on the destination side — without owning them;
(b) finance, coordinate, and book the trade of cacao and agroforestry produce from Northern Brazil (Bahia, Pará, and expansion regions) to buyers abroad, beginning with the United States via the U.S. Import Partner, and prospectively China and Europe;
(c) operate the triangular trade structure authorized by Proposal #18 (Export Partners → Company (Próspera) → Import Partner), booking profit at the Company layer under the Próspera tax regime, on arm's-length terms;
(d) hold treasury assets, administer the TDG-linked NAV buy-back reserve, and support Agroverse and SunMint program objectives; and
(e) do all things a Próspera LLC may lawfully do incident to the foregoing.

3.2 **Mission covenant.** Every material decision of the Company shall be evaluated against the DAO's North Star — whether it advances the restoration of 10,000 hectares of Amazon rainforest in service of healing — and against the DAO's core values of Mindfulness, Compassion, and Equanimity. This covenant is interpretive and aspirational; it does not create third-party rights or override the managers' duties under §3.3.

3.3 **Powers and standard of conduct.** The Company has all powers of a limited liability company under ULLCA. Managers and the Managing Member shall discharge their duties consistent with the duty of loyalty and duty of care as modified by this Agreement to the extent ULLCA permits such modification, and shall at all times observe the human-rights baseline of the Próspera Charter (Art. 14) and applicable RCLC standards.

3.4 **Regulated-activity limitation.** The Company shall not conduct any activity reserved to licensed financial-service providers under the Roatán Financial Services Authority (RFSA) framework (e.g., banking, money transmission, public securities offerings, custody/brokerage of financial instruments) unless and until duly licensed or exempt. TDG and Units shall be administered as internal membership/governance interests and not offered to the public as securities absent compliant structuring (see §5.6 and Article XVII cross-reference to counsel questions).

## Article V — Membership and Units

5.1 **Members = TDG holders.** The Members of the Company are the natural persons and entities recorded on the Main Ledger's *Contributors contact information* tab holding a non-zero TDG balance as of the applicable Snapshot, who have completed the admission requirements of §5.4.

5.2 **Units proportional to TDG.** Membership Units are allocated in proportion to each Member's TDG as of the Snapshot. Illustratively (Brazil Export Entity Brief snapshot, 2026-05-26): 316 contributors held ≈ 2,263,640 TDG. Schedule A records the Units of each Member at the current Snapshot.

5.3 **Snapshot mechanics.** Ownership of record is fixed by periodic **Snapshots** taken on each Seating Date (or on such other cadence as the General Assembly sets by Proposal). Between Snapshots the register is fixed for legal purposes even though the underlying ledger updates continuously. New contributors admitted at the next Snapshot; departures and burns reflected likewise.

5.4 **Admission conditions.** Admission as a Member of record requires: (a) a registered DAO digital signature (public key on file per the DApp identity flow); (b) satisfaction of KYC/beneficial-ownership information the Company reasonably requires for Próspera, Brazilian, and banking compliance; and (c) acceptance of this Agreement (electronic acceptance sufficient, Article XV).

5.5 **1 Unit = 1 vote on entity matters; DAO weighting for DAO matters.** For statutory acts requiring a Member vote *at the Company level* under ULLCA, voting is one-Unit-one-vote (consistent with Proposal #18's "1 share = 1 vote"). For matters routed through the DAO Proposal Process (Article VIII), the DAO's own vote-weighting and eligibility rules apply. Where a matter is both, §8.5 governs reconciliation.

5.6 **Nature of Units; transfer restrictions.** Units are internal governance/economic interests tied to sweat-equity and capital contributions, not instruments offered to the public. Units are **non-transferable** except: (a) by NAV redemption to the Company (Article IX); (b) by operation of the Snapshot re-allocation; or (c) with Board approval and compliance with applicable securities analysis (see counsel questions, LEGAL_ENTITY_STRUCTURING_PROPOSAL_v2.md). No public market for Units is created or endorsed.

5.7 **No personal liability.** No Member, Governor, Manager, or officer is personally liable for the debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being such, consistent with ULLCA and the Próspera Liability Shield Resolution (§ 5-1-183).

## Article VI — Capital Contributions

6.1 **Forms of contribution (per Rubric).** Contributions are recognized as (a) **labor / sweat equity** (100 TDG per hour), (b) **provided funds** (1 TDG per USD of liquidity injected), and (c) in-kind resources as scored by the DAO. All contributions are scored by Edgar, reviewed by Governors, and consolidated to the Main Ledger around solstices/equinoxes.

6.2 **No obligation to contribute further.** No Member is obligated to make additional contributions; dilution follows naturally from others' contributions via the Snapshot mechanism.

6.3 **Fiat injection routing.** External fiat capital intended to finance operating expenses is handled through the designated holding vehicle(s) so as not to distort the contribution/token balance (DAO Fiat Injection policy, 2023-06-28), and, going forward, may be booked in the Company or TrueTech Inc as the Board directs consistent with the triangular structure.

6.4 **Capital accounts.** The Company shall maintain capital-account records reconciled to the Main Ledger and to the Company's financial books (Article XIII).

## Article VII — Governance: The Governor Board (Rotating, 90-Day)

7.1 **Manager-managed.** The Company is manager-managed under ULLCA. The manager is the **Governor Board**.

7.2 **Governors Formula.** The Board is composed, ex officio, of the natural persons seated on the Main Ledger *Governors* tab, which ranks every contributor by total TDG issued over the trailing 180 days (excluding the passive lines "1 TDG for every 1 USDT received" and "−1 TDG for every 1 USDT sent") and seats the top ten (10). There is no campaigning and no self-nomination; the only path onto the Board is sustained contribution.

7.3 **Rotation / re-seating (the 90-day board).** The Board is **automatically re-constituted on each Seating Date** (Spring Equinox, Summer Solstice, Autumn Equinox, Winter Solstice — ≈ every 90 days) to match the then-current Governors tab. A person who leaves the top-ten ranking ceases to be a Manager as of the Seating Date; a person who enters the top ten becomes a Manager as of the Seating Date. The daily-sliding ledger determines *eligibility*; the Seating Date determines *legal seating*, providing a stable quarterly board of record for third parties.

7.4 **Continuity safeguards (the carve-out).** To preserve banking, customs, and counterparty continuity notwithstanding rotation:
(a) the **Managing Member / President** (Article XII) is a separate, non-rotating office and remains the principal authorized signatory across Seating Dates;
(b) authorizations, powers of attorney, banking mandates, and any resident-representative appointments the Company holds for transacting with its Trade Partners survive re-seating until expressly revoked; and
(c) the outgoing Board shall deliver a written handoff (open matters, authorizations, pending Proposals) to the incoming Board within 15 days of each Seating Date.

7.5 **Board authority.** Subject to matters reserved to the Members/General Assembly (§8.4), the Board manages the business and affairs of the Company: approves budgets and AGL terms, negotiates and oversees Trade Partner agreements (Export Partners and the U.S. Import Partner) and the triangular trade terms, administers the buy-back reserve, appoints/removes officers, and executes the DAO's merged proposals at the entity level.

7.6 **Meetings and action.** The Board acts by majority of seated Governors. Meetings may be held electronically; action without a meeting is valid if evidenced by digitally-signed consents of a majority (Article XV). A quorum is a majority of then-seated Governors.

7.7 **Duties.** Governors owe the Company the duties of care and loyalty as modified by this Agreement to the extent ULLCA permits, must avoid undisclosed conflicts, and must uphold the human-rights floor (§4.4). Governors' DAO duties (reviewing Edgar-scored submissions each season; resolving notarization disputes) continue and inform their Company oversight.

7.8 **Vacancies / insufficient Governors.** If fewer than three Governors are seatable, the Managing Member shall convene the General Assembly to fill the Board by Proposal pending the next Seating Date.

## Article VIII — Members, General Assembly, and Voting

8.1 **General Assembly.** Member action is exercised through the DAO's General Assembly / Town Hall, comprising qualifying TDG holders under the DAO's access rules (e.g., ≥100 TDG and recent contribution activity — Whitepaper amendments 2023-09-21A–D).

8.2 **Proposal Process is the voting mechanism.** Member votes at both the DAO and Company level are cast through the **Proposal Process**: digitally-signed submissions via the DApp recorded as pull requests in `github.com/TrueSightDAO/proposals`, tabulated over the voting period, merged on majority-YES and closed on majority-NO (Proposals #12, #15). This is the Company's recognized method of obtaining Member consent under ULLCA and RCLC § 3801 / § 3510.

8.3 **Digital signatures = valid consent.** A digitally-signed vote or consent that verifies against a Member's registered public key constitutes that Member's signature and vote for all purposes of ULLCA and the Entity Registry Statute (Article XV).

8.4 **Reserved matters (Member approval required).** The following require approval through the Proposal Process (majority-YES unless a higher threshold is stated), in addition to any Board action:
(a) amendment of this Agreement or the Certificate of Organization (Article XVIII);
(b) merger, conversion, domestication, or dissolution;
(c) changing the NAV buy-back policy, the Rubric exchange rates, or the AGL fee standard;
(d) issuing Units other than by the Snapshot mechanism, or creating any new class of interest;
(e) terminating or materially restructuring the relationship with a principal Trade Partner (e.g., the U.S. Import Partner, or an Export Partner representing a material share of trade flow);
(f) admitting external equity investors into the Company; and
(g) any matter the Board refers to the Members.

8.5 **Reconciliation of entity vote vs. DAO vote.** Where a reserved matter is also a statutory act requiring a one-Unit-one-vote tally under ULLCA, the Managing Member shall run both tallies; the action passes only if it satisfies **both** the DAO Proposal Process outcome and the ULLCA one-Unit-one-vote threshold. Absent conflict, the Proposal Process outcome governs.

8.6 **Record of Member action.** Merged proposals and their digitally-signed vote records are the Company's minute book for Member action and are incorporated into Schedule C as they accrue.

## Article IX — Distributions and NAV Buy-Back / Redemption

9.1 **Distributions discretionary.** Distributions, if any, are declared by the Board consistent with the DAO's treasury policy and Próspera solvency/financial-responsibility rules (Próspera Financial Responsibility Statute, § 2-2-76). No Member has a right to a distribution except as declared.

9.2 **Ledger-based buy-back reserve.** Consistent with **Proposal #14** (migration away from Raydium toward a ledger-based buy-back program), the Company maintains a USD buy-back reserve recorded on the ledger, funded by budget the DAO allocates and by surplus routed from operations (including TrueTech Inc subscription/licensing surplus that boosts NAV).

9.3 **Redemption at NAV.** A Member may request redemption ("withdraw voting rights") through the DApp; the reserve is debited and the corresponding TDG/Units are burned at the then-current NAV, subject to available reserves. Buy-backs are **discretionary, not guaranteed**; if reserves are insufficient, redemptions queue and fill as revenue arrives (LEGAL_ENTITY_STRUCTURING_PROPOSAL_v2.md, "Buyback Capacity"). This mirrors Proposal #18's "shares sold back to the company at NAV."

9.4 **Early-exit penalty for managed-ledger equity.** Where a Member holds an equity position in a managed AGL and exits prematurely, that managed-ledger position is reclaimed at a significant discount to the initial contribution, as a penalty for early exit. This penalty applies to managed-ledger equity only, **not** to governance Units earned for contributions (Whitepaper, Treasury §).

## Article X — AGL Shipment Financing and the 20% Fee

10.1 **Standard fee.** For all **future** AGL contracts that are **shipment financing** (direct shipment with physical collateral), the default DAO fund-management fee is **20%** of post-recoup profit, unless a specific contract states otherwise (**Proposal #17**, merged 2026-02-24; Agroverse Whitepaper, Tokenomics §).

10.2 **No double-charging.** **Operational-fund** AGLs (ledgers that invest in other AGLs) charge **0%** at that layer, because the underlying AGLs already bear the 20% fee.

10.3 **Financier options.** A shipment financier may (a) hold for profit — capital repaid first, remaining profit split 80% financier / 20% DAO — or (b) exchange the position for the physical inventory it represents, bearing shipping cost and the DAO management fee on cost of goods (Agroverse Whitepaper, Role 0).

10.4 **Existing contracts.** Contracts predating Proposal #17 keep their prior terms unless amended by mutual agreement.

10.5 **Securities note.** The AGL shipment-financing pattern may raise a separate securities question (Reves promissory-note / investment-contract analysis) distinct from the Units analysis; the Board shall structure AGL offerings on advice of counsel and within any applicable RFSA exemption.

## Article XI — Trade Partner Network and Triangular Trade Structure

11.1 **Partner network, not ownership.** The Company operates through a network of **independent Trade Partners** and does **not** own, control, or consolidate any of them. Each Export Partner (Black King, Coopercabruca, CEPOTX, and future partners) is a Brazilian legal person holding its own foreign-trade credentials (CNPJ, Inscrição Estadual, RADAR / Portal Único Siscomex, NF-e); the U.S. Import Partner (TrueTech Inc) is an independent Delaware corporation. In the eyes of the Honduran/Próspera authorities, these are the Company's contractual counterparties on the origin and destination sides, respectively.

11.2 **Partner agreements.** The Company contracts with each Trade Partner on arm's-length terms via written partner/supply/purchase agreements (which may be digitally signed, Article XV). Such agreements define pricing, quality, delivery, evidence/traceability, and, where applicable, the AGL shipment-financing terms (Article X). No agreement shall characterize a Partner as a subsidiary, employee, or general agent of the Company.

11.3 **Triangular trade.** Trade flows Export Partner (Brazil) → Company (Próspera) → Import Partner (US) → retailers, with profit booked at the Company layer under the Próspera regime, structured on arm's-length terms and to comply with Brazilian and U.S. cross-border/transfer-pricing rules (Brazil Export Entity Brief, §5). Because the Company transacts as a purchaser/reseller rather than as a quotaholder, it avoids the foreign-ownership question raised for a Company-owned Ltda.

11.4 **FSVP and traceability continuity.** TrueTech Inc remains the U.S. FDA-FSVP and Customs importer of record; the Company and its Export Partners shall preserve supplier verification, FDA Food Facility Registrations, and traceability documentation for Coopercabruca, CEPOTX, Black King, and other verified suppliers (`github.com/TrueSightDAO/fda_fsvp`).

11.5 **Partner admission and removal.** The Board may admit or remove Trade Partners in the ordinary course; admitting a Partner does not make it a Member of the Company, and a Member's status as a Partner (or a Partner's principal being a Member) does not enlarge that Member's Units. Related-party dealings (e.g., where a Partner's principal is also a Member or Governor) shall be disclosed and approved consistent with §7.7 (conflicts).

11.6 **Additional markets.** China and Europe Import Partners may be added by Board action within the purposes of §3.1.

## Article XIV — Tax Matters

14.1 **Próspera tax regime.** The Company is subject to Próspera Business Income Tax at an effective **1%** of business income (Tax Statute 2019; Overview of Taxation), due April 30 annually, plus Retail VAT (effective 2.5%) only on final retail sales made within Próspera's spatial competence, if any. Próspera imposes no capital-gains, import/export duty, payroll, or withholding beyond those specified by the Tax Statute (see BPN No. 8 for extraterritorial-investment treatment).

14.2 **Filings and payment.** Filings and payment are made via the e-Próspera platform; qualifying cryptocurrencies are accepted (§ 5-1-139; RFSA AA No. 10 — cryptocurrencies as monetary instruments).

14.3 **Cross-border.** Each Trade Partner is responsible for its own Brazilian or U.S. tax obligations; the Company's purchase/resale pricing with its Partners is documented at arm's length per §11.2. Nothing here is tax advice; the Board shall obtain qualified cross-border tax counsel.

## Article XV — Digital Signatures and Electronic Records

15.1 **UETA / UCC-12 validity.** Electronic records and electronic/digital signatures have the same legal effect as writings and manual signatures under RCLC § 3801 (UETA) and § 3510 (UCC Article 12 — Controllable Electronic Records). The Members intend all Company governance to be conductable electronically.

15.2 **DAO key = binding signature.** A submission (vote, consent, resolution, or filing authorization) that cryptographically verifies against a person's registered DAO public key is that person's authenticated signature and act for the Company, the Registrar, and third parties relying in good faith.

## Article XVII — Dispute Resolution

17.1 **Próspera Arbitration.** Any dispute arising under this Agreement or the Company's affairs shall be resolved by arbitration under the **Próspera Arbitration Statute 2019** (§ 2-1-37) administered by the **Próspera Arbitration Center** (`pac.hn`), seated in Próspera ZEDE, conducted in English (with Spanish translation as required), by one arbitrator unless the amount in controversy warrants three.

17.2 **Internal escalation first.** Members shall first use the DAO's internal dispute path where applicable — e.g., a contributor's right to surface a scoring/notarization dispute to the Town Hall for General Assembly vote (Whitepaper, Dispute resolution §) — before commencing arbitration on governance-scoring matters.

17.3 **Governing substantive law.** The RCLC and Próspera statutes govern; the Restatements and Uniform Acts adopted into the RCLC (e.g., Contracts, Agency, LLC Act) supply the rules of decision.

17.4 **Human-rights forum.** Nothing forecloses recourse to the Próspera Human Rights Defense Committee for matters within its competence.

## Article XIX — Dissolution and Winding Up

19.1 **Triggers.** The Company dissolves upon (a) a reserved-matter Member vote to dissolve; (b) entry of a judicial/administrative dissolution; or (c) an event making its purpose unlawful or impossible.

19.2 **Winding up.** The Managing Member (or a Board-appointed liquidator) winds up: satisfies creditors, settles open balances with Trade Partners and any intercompany positions, and distributes any surplus to Members in proportion to Units at the final Snapshot, after the buy-back reserve and managed-ledger obligations are addressed.

19.3 **Filings.** Articles of Dissolution and any Statement of Withdrawal of foreign registrations are filed with the Registrar and any relevant Brazilian/U.S. authorities.
