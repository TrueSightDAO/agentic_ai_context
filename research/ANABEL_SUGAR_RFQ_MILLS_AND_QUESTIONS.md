## PART 1: INFORMATION WE NEED FROM ANABEL

Before we can approach the sugar mills in Brazil with a credible inquiry, we need the following details from Anabel:

### 1. Sugar Grade (ICUMSA & Granulation)
- Which ICUMSA grade? ICUMSA 45 (premium white), ICUMSA 150, ICUMSA 600–1200 (raw)?
- Which granulation? **Sugar #5** (caster/superfine, ~250–400 µm) or **Sugar #45** (icing/powdered, ~75–150 µm)?
  - Note: Both #5 and #45 can be ICUMSA 45 grade — the difference is particle size, not purity.
  - Sugar #45 is finer and costs more to produce.

### 2. Destination Port in China
- CIF pricing varies significantly by port: Shanghai, Shenzhen, Tianjin, Qingdao, etc.
- Mills cannot quote freight without knowing the destination.

### 3. Contract Duration
- 50,000 tons/month for how long? 6 months? 12 months? 24 months?
- Mills price differently for short-term vs long-term commitments.

### 4. Payment Terms
- LC at sight? Deferred LC? SBLC?
- Minimum LC size per shipment?

### 5. Packaging
- 50 kg bags? 1-ton jumbo bags? Bulk vessel?
- Affects pricing significantly.

### 6. Inspection & Certification
- Does China require specific certifications (CIQ inspection)?
- SGS inspection at loading port needed?

### 7. Timeline
- When does she need the first delivery?
- Mills typically need 30–60 days lead time for production scheduling.

## PART 2: BRAZILIAN SUGAR MILLS — RESEARCH COMPILATION

### Background

Per our research (Milena's intel + web research), there are approximately **8 major sugar mills in Brazil** that produce ICUMSA 45 / refined white sugar. The minimum volume mills will consider for an LC is **12,500–25,000 tons/month**. Anabel's request for **50,000 tons/month** is well above that threshold and will be taken seriously.

**Important note on Sugar #5 vs Sugar #45:**
- Both are the **same ICUMSA 45 refined white sugar** — the difference is **granulation size**.
- Sugar #5 = caster/superfine sugar (~250–400 µm, 40–60 mesh)
- Sugar #45 = icing/powdered sugar (~75–150 µm, 100–200 mesh)
- Sugar #45 is simply Sugar #5 ground finer. **The same mills can produce both** if they have the milling equipment.
- Mills that list "Extra Fine Crystal Sugar" or "Sugar Powder" in their product lines can definitely produce Sugar #45.

**Our approach:** We will approach the mills as **TrueTech Inc.**, a USA FDA-registered importer, presenting the purchase case. This gives us credibility with the mills.
